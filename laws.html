There are 2 big considerations - evolution of technology and the fact that digital information flows freely all around the globe, over the lines of the internet. Information Technology changes fast, and the law struggles to keep up with the pace. so Tort Law is often used by litigants, with signed contracts being used by the accused IT provider, as defence in cases of omissions or harmful impact of related software. A tort is anything that causes injury or harm to another and amounts to a civil wrong for which courts impose liability. In tort law, injury is invasion of any legal right, and harm is a loss or detriment suffered.

Mal-functioning software and/or hardware has been a topic in tort law cases relating to IT.

Contracts are legally binding agreements.

IT providers typically use a contract to define the expectations of a product or service, adding disclaimers if necessary, to protect the organization and staff.

Zetabytes (1 ZB = trillion GB) of personal and business data are stored digitally,with associated privacy concerns.

Constitutional law defines the role, powers, and structure of the executive, the parliament or legislature, and the judiciary. In the context of IT, an example is the treatment of freedom of speech on the internet.

Cybercrime, crime committed on the internet or through the use of digital devices, poses a threat to privacy and security. The impact of cybercrime is argued under tort and constitutional law.

Intellectual property describes works of the human intellect, like art, literature and software. In terms of Legal Tech, the focus is on digital works, notably software. Intellectual property is often protected by patents, trademarks, and copyrights.

A copyright is a type of intellectual property that supports the creator in ownership of a creative work, with exclusive rights to determine how that work is used and distributed.

Copyright protection describes the legal ramifications of infringements on copyright. Prosecution under the law, involving financial and criminal penalties apply.

The copyright symbol, or copyright sign, ©, is the symbol used in copyright notices for works other than sound recordings. The Universal Copyright Convention created this symbol.

A doctrines is a set of rules or principles with a long history in the law. When a set of rules is termed a doctrine, it is important to some field of law, or it provides a rule based way to resolve a legal dispute.

Let's start with a bill - when a bill is passed by the Senate and the House, and the president signs it, the bill becomes an Act.

When criteria for enforcing an Act are created, the Act becomes a Law. Laws are also known as Acts of Congress. An Act is passed by the legislative branch, whereas in a somewhat tenuous difference between Act and Law, Law is the set of rules and regulations that the government enforces.

The Digital Millennium Copyright Act was created in 1998, as an addendum to United States copyright law. It criminalizes devices or services that circumvent measures to control access to copyrighted works.

The United States No Electronic Theft Act, is a federal law passed in 1997. Under this act copyright infringement is prosecuted, even when there is no monetary profit or commercial benefit from the infringement.

Under certain circumstances, the unauthorized use of copyrighted materials is ok, when the infringer's use qualifies as fair use. Fair Use is judged by 4 factors:

  • purpose of the use, commercial? nonprofit educational?
  • nature of the copyrighted work
  • amount of the copyrighted work used
  • effect of the use upon the market or value of the copyrighted work

COPPA, Children's Online Privacy Protection Act, is targeted at children under the age of 13. It imposes requirements on websites and online services that collect personal information from children.

The Children's Internet Protection Act (CIPA) was enacted in 2000 to prevent children's access to harmful content on the Internet. CIPA imposes requirements on schools or libraries that receive discounts for Internet access or internal connections through federal programs. Schools and libraries subject to CIPA must certify that the Internet safety policy filters Internet access to obscene, harmful and pornographic content.

Total guesses needed to guess a password. Calculate entropy, using
L – The required length of the password
R – The required range of characters
For passwords with only lower case letters, R = 26. If the password must contain lower, upper and digits, R = 62 (26 + 26 + 10)

RL is large, so entropy is defined as the binary log of the value.

entropy (E): E = log2(RL)

Home routers use network address translation (NAT) to share a single IP address from the ISP among computers in your house. A NAT firewall is able to assess internet traffic and block unsolicited communications. It only accepts web traffic if a device on the house network solicited it.

Every IP address contains two kinds of ports, TCP and UDP, and there can be up to 65,535 of each for any given IP address. Services that connect to the internet (like web browsers, email clients, and file transfer services).
Any internet-connected service requires specific ports to be open in order to function. These can be exploited by malware, cybercriminals use open ports to access a computer. Close any ports that aren’t associated with a known legitimate service!

An international mobile subscriber identity-catcher, or IMSI-catcher, is a telephone eavesdropping device used for intercepting mobile phone traffic and tracking location data of mobile phone users. The device poses as a fake mobile tower between the target mobile phone and the service provider's real towers. The 3G and the LTE wireless standards require authentication from both the handset and the network, but attacks may be able to downgrade 3G and LTE to non-LTE network services which do not require mutual authentication.
